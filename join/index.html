Work with me · University of Miami · Electrical & Computer Engineering

Prospective students and collaborators

Starting in Fall 2026, I’ll be recruiting PhD students, postdocs, visitors, interns, and collaborators at the University of Miami. The group will work on secure AI agent systems and the evidence infrastructure around them: how agents use data, fail, get audited, and get fixed after something goes wrong.

I am especially interested in people who are skeptical of easy claims and can turn a vague failure mode into a threat model, dataset, benchmark, disclosure workflow, protocol, proof, or working system.

Email CV + short note → Details below.

Fit

What I want to see

The homepage has the research agenda. This page is about fit.

Problem taste

You can turn a pitfall into a study

Data leakage, benchmark overfitting, prompt injection, bad disclosure channels, brittle tool use, unclear consent: pick a real failure and make it measurable.

Artifacts

You have work I can inspect

A paper, repo, demo, dataset, benchmark, audit, dashboard, proof, bug write-up, design doc, or system someone used. It does not have to be famous.

Collaboration

People can depend on your work

I care about clear writing, good judgment, and evidence that you can carry a project when other people depend on your work.

Background

I am open on background

Security, ML, systems, cryptography, data engineering, HCI, PL, theory, and policy-aware technical work can all fit. The common thread is wanting to understand where agent systems fail.

Openings

Paths into the group

Different paths make sense at different stages. The categories are practical, not rigid.

PhD students · Spring & Fall 2027

PhD students

Fully funded PhD positions through the College of Engineering. Prior work on agents is not required. Strong background or judgment in security, ML, systems, cryptography, HCI, PL, or theory is enough to start.

Postdocs · now

Postdocs

For people who want to help define the first research questions. Send one or two representative projects and the question you would want to own.

Visiting students & interns · now

Visiting students & interns

For current PhD or MS students who want to collaborate for a semester or summer, remotely or in Miami. Timing and scope matter more than a long proposal.

Research collaborators · now

Research collaborators

For faculty, industry researchers, and builders. The best fit is a project with a clear technical question and a path to a paper, system, benchmark, or useful public artifact.

Apply

Next steps

Email first

A useful first email has three things: who you are, one piece of work I should look at, and one concrete problem you want to think about. Long generic emails are less useful.

PhD applicants should also apply to the program

In addition to sending the note, apply to the University of Miami College of Engineering PhD program and name Guy Zyskind as a potential advisor. For Spring 2027, reach out as early as you can. For Fall 2027, apply during the coming cycle.

Postdocs, visitors, interns, and collaborators

A direct email is enough to start. Include what you want to work on, your timing, and what kind of collaboration you have in mind.
