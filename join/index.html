Work with me · University of Miami · Electrical & Computer Engineering

Research openings

At the University of Miami, I am starting a research group on the security of agentic AI. The practical question is simple: as software moves from humans clicking buttons to agents transacting with agents, what has to be true for people and organizations to trust the result? I am recruiting the first PhD students, postdocs, visiting students, interns, and collaborators. Strong fits can show evidence of careful collaboration: a paper, repo, system, demo, write-up, or project that other people relied on.

Research directions

Problems on the table

This list is intentionally open. I am looking for people who can help turn broad questions into precise systems, experiments, and theory.

Agent protocols

Trust between agents

Identity, authorization, commitments, and accountability when agents represent different users, companies, or institutions.

Verification

Evidence after action

Ways for agents to prove which data and tools they used, what policy they followed, and where human review is still needed.

Data boundaries

Organizational data leakage

How agents can work with source code, customer data, credentials, and internal strategy without turning private context into shared context.

Social influence

Agent persuasion and manipulation

How susceptible agents are to pressure, framing, impersonation, and collusion, and how protocols can make those risks visible.

Open AI

Open and decentralized models

How open models and decentralized AI infrastructure can improve access and inspectability while preserving privacy and security.

Cryptography

Confidential and verifiable computation

Applying FHE, MPC, TEEs, commitments, and zero-knowledge techniques where they solve real agent-security problems.

Who I’m looking for

Four ways in

Different stages, same bar: curiosity, taste in problems, and evidence you can make progress with other people.

Research collaborators · now

Research collaborators

Faculty, industry researchers, and builders working on agent security, verifiable AI, privacy, open AI infrastructure, or applied cryptography. I am open to projects with a real technical question and a path to publish or build.

What makes a strong reach-out. A good note makes it easy to see how you think and what it might be like to work together. I care more about concrete evidence than pedigree: a paper, repo, system, demo, write-up, or project where other people depended on you. The best messages include:

  • • Your CV or a link to it.
  • • 2-3 sentences on a concrete problem in agentic-AI security you’d want to work on.
  • • A link to something you’re proud of: a repo, paper, system, demo, or write-up.

You do not need to match my current list exactly. The point of the group is to define the area carefully.

How to apply

Three steps

Send a short note

Email your CV, 2-3 sentences on a concrete problem, and one link to work you are proud of. Everyone, including PhD, postdoc, visiting, intern, and collaborator candidates, can start here.

PhD applicants: apply to the program

In addition to sending the note, apply to the University of Miami College of Engineering PhD program and name Guy Zyskind as a potential advisor. For Fall 2027, apply during the coming cycle; for Spring 2027, reach out as early as you can.

Postdocs, visitors & collaborators: let’s talk

A direct email is enough to start. Include what you would like to work on and your ideal timing.
