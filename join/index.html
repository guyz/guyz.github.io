Work with me · University of Miami · Electrical & Computer Engineering

Join the group

I’m starting a research group on the security of agentic AI — how autonomous agents interact, coordinate, transact, and act on our behalf without breaking the things we care about. It’s a wide-open field, and I’m building the founding team now. If you’re excited about cutting-edge research and you have evidence of being a great collaborator, I want to hear from you.

Fill the interest form → …or email guy@fhenix.io with your CV + 3 sentences.

Who I’m looking for

Four ways in

Different stages, same bar: curiosity, rigor, and a track record of building or proving things.

PhD students · Spring & Fall 2027

PhD students

Fully-funded PhD positions through the College of Engineering. Backgrounds in security, cryptography, machine learning, systems, or theory are all welcome — what matters is drive and fit. See the steps below to apply.

Postdocs · now

Postdocs

If you want to help define a new area at the intersection of AI and security, let’s talk. Reach out with your CV and a short note on the problems you’d want to own.

Visiting students & interns · now

Visiting students & interns

Current PhD or MS students who want to collaborate for a semester or a summer — remotely or in Miami. Tell me what you’d like to work on and for how long.

Research collaborators · now

Research collaborators

Faculty, industry researchers, and builders working on agent security, verifiable AI, or applied cryptography. I care more about ambition and rigor than about titles.

What makes a strong reach-out. I weight evidence of being a great collaborator — things you’ve built, shipped, proven, or published — over pedigree. The best messages include:

  • • Your CV or a link to it.
  • • 2–3 sentences on a concrete problem in agentic-AI security you’d want to work on.
  • • A link to something you’re proud of — a repo, a paper, a system, a demo, a write-up.

You don’t need to have worked on all — or any — of my exact topics. Show me how you think and what you can do.

How to apply

Three steps

Fill the interest form

It takes a few minutes and is the surest way to reach me without getting lost in an inbox. Everyone — PhD, postdoc, visiting, or collaborator — should start here.

PhD applicants: apply to the program

In addition to the form, apply to the University of Miami College of Engineering PhD program and name Guy Zyskind as a potential advisor. For Fall 2027, get your application in during the coming cycle; for Spring 2027, reach out as early as you can.

Postdocs, visitors & collaborators: let’s talk

The form (or a direct email) is enough — we can move quickly. Include what you’d like to work on and your ideal timing.
