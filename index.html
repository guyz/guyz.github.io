Research

The security of agentic AI

Soon software won’t just answer us — it will act for us: booking, buying, negotiating, and coordinating with other agents across systems no person fully sees. My group studies how to make that world trustworthy. I bring a cryptographer’s toolkit — verifiable, confidential, and adversarial thinking — to questions that are still wide open. A few of the directions I’m most excited about:

Agent ↔ agent

Trust between agents

When your software negotiates, delegates, and transacts with software you’ve never met, what do identity, authorization, and accountability even mean? I want protocols that let agents cooperate without having to trust one another.

Verification

Proving an agent behaved

How do we know an autonomous agent did what it claimed — and nothing more? Attestation, verifiable execution, and guardrails that hold up under adversarial pressure, not just in the demo.

User protection

Protecting you and your AI

Your agent will hold your keys, your data, and your intent. Keeping all of it private, revocable, and genuinely under your control as it acts across an open world of other agents.

Cryptography × AI

A cryptographic toolkit for AI

Bringing FHE, secure computation, TEEs, and zero-knowledge proofs to bear on agent security — so safety becomes something you can prove, not just hope for.

Track record

A decade turning cryptography into systems people actually use

2015

The first confidential smart contracts

MIT · Enigma

As part of my doctoral work I co-authored the first proposal to combine secure multi-party computation with blockchains, so programs could run on data that stays encrypted. It has been cited thousands of times and is widely recognized as the starting point for confidential smart contracts.

Enigma paper IEEE S&P WIRED
2020

Confidential smart contracts, in production

Secret Network

I launched the first live network to run smart contracts with encrypted inputs, outputs, and state. It proved confidential computation could work at blockchain scale and secured billions in value; SecretSwap pioneered front-running- and MEV-resistant trading.

CoinDesk · $400M Blockworks
2021

Cryptography that made headlines

Tarantino · Kevin Smith

Encrypted “Secret NFTs” with Quentin Tarantino released unseen Pulp Fiction material, and Kevin Smith’s KillRoy Was Here became the first full-length feature film minted as an NFT — with global coverage from Bloomberg to Fortune.

Bloomberg Fortune Deadline

FHE for Ethereum — and a doctorate

Fhenix · MIT

I founded Fhenix to bring fully homomorphic encryption to Ethereum, raising a $15M Series A, and completed my PhD at MIT on secure computation in decentralized systems.
