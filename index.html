NEWS: Starting in Fall 2026, I’m joining the University of Miami as an Assistant Professor of Electrical & Computer Engineering. I’m also the Founder of Fhenix, where we’re building secure infrastructure for institutions bringing high-value assets onchain.

My background sits between research and company-building: I co-authored the first work on confidential smart contracts, founded privacy infrastructure companies that raised more than $60M, and helped move private computation from MIT research into live networks, institutional infrastructure, and public applications. At Miami, I’ll focus on the next hard problem: the trust layer for a world where AI agents transact with other agents.

Research Agenda

The security of agentic AI

As software shifts from humans clicking buttons to agents interacting with agents, many of the security assumptions behind today’s web stop fitting. Agents carry organizational data, call tools, negotiate, and make commitments across boundaries. My group will study the trust layer for that setting: how agents should identify themselves, what they are allowed to reveal or retain, and how people can verify what happened after the fact. A few directions I want to explore:

Agent protocols

Agent-to-agent trust

When agents represent different users, companies, or institutions, how should they authenticate intent, delegate authority, and leave evidence after a transaction? The goal is cooperation without assuming the other agent is benign.

Verification

Evidence of behavior

Can an agent give useful evidence about what information it read, which tools it used, and whether it stayed within policy, without exposing the private content itself? I care about auditability that still works under adversarial use.

Data boundaries

Keeping organizational data in bounds

How do organizations let agents do useful work without leaking source code, customer data, credentials, or strategy when an agent misreads context, over-shares, or gets manipulated?

Social influence

When agents persuade agents

AI agents may be easier to influence than we expect, especially when other agents can frame requests, apply pressure, impersonate authority, or collude. I want to study those failure modes in a precise way.

User protection

Control for users

Personal agents will hold data, keys, preferences, and intent. Users will need ways to grant authority, revoke it, understand what happened, and keep their private context from becoming general infrastructure.

Open and Decentralized AI

Open and decentralized models

Open models and decentralized infrastructure can keep AI capability more accessible and inspectable. The research question is how to get those benefits without giving up privacy, accountability, or security.

Research that became companies, networks, and production systems

Confidential computation on blockchains

MIT · Enigma

At MIT, I co-authored the first proposal for combining secure multi-party computation with blockchains: run programs on data that stays private, then settle the result on a public network. That line of work has been cited thousands of times and became the starting point for Enigma.

Secret Network · SCRT Labs

Led the launch of Secret Network, the first mainnet to run smart contracts with encrypted inputs, outputs, and state. The network reached unicorn-scale market value, secured billions in onchain value, and later announced a $400M ecosystem initiative for builders.

Encrypted media with public attention

Secret Network · Tarantino · Kevin Smith

Secret NFTs used encryption and access control for high-profile media drops, including Quentin Tarantino’s unseen Pulp Fiction material and Kevin Smith’s KillRoy Was Here. The point was not the NFT label; it was showing that public networks could handle private, rights-aware content.
